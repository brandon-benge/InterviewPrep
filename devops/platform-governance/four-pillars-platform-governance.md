# Four Pillars of Platform Governance

## Security Governance — Who is allowed to do what?

- Security  — Identity, access, network isolation, secrets, image policies, and audit controls.
- Compliance / Data  — Data classification, encryption, retention, audit evidence, and tenant isolation.

## Operational Governance — How do we ensure reliability and recoverability?

- Operations — Service levels, observability, backup, disaster recovery, and incident management.
- Developer Experience — Self-service, documentation, scorecards, workflows, and exception management.
- AI-Enabled Platform Operations  — Incident analysis

## Financial Governance — How do we control cost and capacity?

- Resource Cost  — Quotas, limits, autoscaling, chargeback, and retention policies.
- AI-Enabled Platform Cost — infer ownership and cost optimization

## Lifecycle Governance — How do we standardize, upgrade, and deprecate safely?

- Deployment / Change  — GitOps, approvals, testing, release strategies, upgrades, and rollback standards.
- Standards  — Naming conventions, templates, service catalog, ownership, and lifecycle policies.
- AI-Enabled Platform Lifecycle — Upgrade/Deprecations recommendations and build scorecards.
